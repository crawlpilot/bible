## Choosing the Right Method

| Context | Method | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Early roadmap, many unknowns | T-shirt sizing | Fast, good enough for prioritization |
| Sprint planning, known backlog | Story points + planning poker | Team-aligned, surfaces disagreement |
| Project with hard deadline | PERT (3-point) | Quantifies schedule risk explicitly |
| Large initiative, multiple teams | Complexity mapping + PERT | Forces dependency and risk identification |
| Executive briefing | PERT output → confidence intervals | Gives range, not false precision |

## The Estimation Mindset at Principal Level

- **Estimates are ranges, not dates.** "8–13 weeks at 70% confidence" is more honest than "10 weeks."
- **Estimation reveals assumptions.** The value of an estimation session is the conversation, not the number.
- **Scope is the variable, not time.** When a deadline is fixed, adjust scope — not quality.
- **Uncertainty shrinks with discovery.** Phase 0 (spike/discovery) always reduces estimation error more than any technique.
- **Track actuals.** Estimation calibration is only possible if you measure prediction vs. reality over time.
