# Agile Scrum — Principal Engineer Guide

Scrum is a delivery mechanism, not a religion. At principal level, the point is not to "do Scrum correctly"; it is to use sprint-based execution to reduce risk, expose dependencies early, and create a reliable planning cadence for the team and its stakeholders.

## Principal Engineer Principles

- Sprint commitments should reflect capacity and uncertainty, not optimism.
- Velocity is a trailing indicator of team output, not a target to maximize.
- Retrospectives should produce one or two concrete experiments, not a long wish list.
- Cross-team dependencies are a planning problem, not a ceremony problem.
- If the cadence is producing false confidence, the process is wrong even if every ceremony is "green."

## What Good Looks Like

- The sprint goal is understandable by engineers, PMs, and stakeholders.
- The backlog is small enough that top items can be discussed in detail.
- Unplanned work is visible and explicitly traded off against planned scope.
- The team can explain why it missed a commitment without blaming individuals.
- Retrospectives create follow-up work with owners and deadlines.
- Ceremonies run on timebox — standups are 15 min, planning is ≤ 2 hours.
- DORA metrics are tracked: deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, MTTR.
