# Leadership & Behavioral

Engineering leadership content calibrated to principal engineer scope. Every story should demonstrate org-level impact, ambiguity navigation, and influence without authority.

## Sub-directories

| Folder | Contents |
|--------|----------|
| `sstar-examples/` | SSTAR behavioral stories (Situation→Strategy→Task→Action→Result) |
| `manager-frameworks/` | Coaching, performance management, feedback models (SBI, radical candor) |
| `team-building/` | Hiring bar-raising, onboarding, psychological safety, culture |
| `principal-engineer-skills/` | Technical vision, RFC writing, driving org-wide decisions, eng/product boundaries |

## SSTAR Format (Principal Engineer calibration)

```
**Situation**: Set the stage — scope, stakes, who was involved
**Strategy**: Your approach to the ambiguous problem (what lens you used)
**Task**: Specifically what YOU were responsible for
**Action**: Concrete steps, decisions made, trade-offs navigated
**Result**: Quantified impact — team, org, or company level
```

Use `/sstar [scenario]` with Claude to generate or coach an existing story.

## Key Themes for Principal Engineer Bar

- Technical direction for teams of 20+ engineers
- Ambiguous, cross-functional problems with no clear owner
- Influencing decisions without direct authority
- Building systems or processes that outlive your involvement
- Identifying and resolving org-level technical debt or risk
- Navigating engineering vs product boundaries: ownership, conflict resolution, communication
